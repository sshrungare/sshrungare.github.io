With the above discussion in mind, it will be clearly helpful to predict “How Many days a patient may stay in the hospital for treatment?”

The length of stay (LOS)—the number of days between patient admission and discharge —is essential to a healthcare facility. LOS varies significantly because of more variable parameters such as illness conditions and specializations available at hospitals etc. This makes manually planning patients' lengths of stay in the hospital challenging.

This process can be automated to make all resources at the hospital are used efficiently. 

System Architecture – 

When patients are admitted, ML models are used to predict their length of stay. This approach can be used by hospital administrators to optimize resource consumption and capacity planning. They can determine which facilities are overwhelmed and which resources within those facilities should be increased. It can also be used to determine whether there are sufficient staff resources to handle a patient's release.

Stage 1 -

Data for EHR / EMR (Electronic Health Records / Electronic Medical Records) is derived from sources such as databases, flat files, and FIHR. Other data attributes, such as available/total hospital resources, can also be extracted or input into the system via the web portal or care manager portal.

Stage 2 -

To ingest data from various sources to the data lake, we can utilize SparkSQL, PySpark, and SPARK Streaming. Data purification activities can be performed here to feed data to the ML model.

Stage 3 -

Data that has been cleaned can be fed into a pre-trained ML model. For prediction, it can be manually triggered, scheduled, or event-based.

Stage 4 -

Predicted data, together with other patient data, can be displayed on a dashboard or RTMS such as Apache Superset. This information can then be used by the care manager to make the best use of available resources while also preparing for future hospitalizations.



Predicting the length of stay is advantageous to patients, their families, and insurance companies. A hospital discharge date helps patients and their families identify and predict medical expenditures.
